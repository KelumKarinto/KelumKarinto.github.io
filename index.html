About Me

Welcome to my homepage! I am Kelum Chamara Manoj Lakmal Elvitigala, currently associated with the Graduate School of Engineering Science at Osaka University. My research focuses on the intersection of biotechnology and materials science, specifically in the development of hydrogels and their applications in tissue engineering and regenerative medicine.

I have extensively worked on tuning the crosslinking and degradation properties of hyaluronic acid/gelatin hydrogels using hydrogen peroxide, which is pivotal for fabricating muscle cell sheets and modulating cell-cycle progression. My work has contributed to understanding how these hydrogels can influence cell adhesion, proliferation, and differentiation, which are crucial for creating effective biomaterials for medical applications​ (AD Scientific Index)​​ (MDPI)​​ (RSC Publishing)​.

Feel free to explore my publications and research contributions through the links provided. If you have any inquiries or wish to collaborate, please don't hesitate to contact me via email or phone.
